# Review Gate: Before Draft

## Purpose

Use this advisory checkpoint to decide whether the article is ready to move from planning into drafting. The gate helps the writer, reviewer, or assisting agent confirm that the intent, context pack, angle, and structure are coherent enough to support a focused first draft.

This gate is document-first and human-confirmed. It is not a runtime-enforced blocker, hidden automation step, model-call requirement, or platform-specific approval mechanism.

## When To Use

Use this gate after the planning artifacts are prepared and before drafting begins, especially when:

- the topic is complex, sensitive, or easy to over-broaden;
- the audience, thesis, or angle may still be unclear;
- the structure has changed since the context pack was assembled;
- a writer or agent is about to hand off from planning to drafting.

Skip or keep the check lightweight for very small notes where the user has already confirmed the direction and no separate structure is needed.

## Input Artifacts

Recommended inputs:

- `intent`: topic, audience, purpose, constraints, and success criteria.
- `context-pack`: source material, relevant background, assumptions, risks, and exclusions.
- `angle`: working thesis, reader promise, framing, and non-goals.
- `structure`: proposed outline, section flow, evidence placement, and unresolved questions.

If an artifact is intentionally absent, record why the remaining planning material is still enough to draft safely.

## Advisory Checklist

Before drafting, check:

- Intent is specific enough to guide content decisions.
- Audience and reader needs are visible and consistent across planning artifacts.
- The context pack separates reliable source material from assumptions, speculation, and open questions.
- The angle states a clear thesis or reader promise rather than only a topic label.
- The structure supports the angle and does not introduce major unsupported claims.
- Important constraints, exclusions, or sensitive areas are documented before drafting begins.
- Any unresolved questions are either answered, explicitly deferred, or marked as draft-time risks.
- The planned draft can be written without inventing missing facts or silently expanding scope.

## User Confirmation / Decision Record

Record the user's decision in the planning notes, task thread, or other visible handoff location.

Suggested format:

```md
### Before Draft Gate Decision

- Decision: Proceed to draft / Revise planning first / Pause for clarification
- Confirmed by: <user or accountable reviewer>
- Date: <YYYY-MM-DD>
- Inputs reviewed: intent, context-pack, angle, structure, <other>
- Known caveats: <open questions, assumptions, or deferred checks>
- Drafting instructions: <tone, structure, exclusions, or handoff notes>
```

Proceeding means the user accepts the current planning basis for drafting. It does not mean every fact is final, and it does not remove the need for later review.

## If Not Ready

If the gate is not ready, choose the smallest useful follow-up:

- tighten the intent or audience statement;
- add missing context, evidence, or source notes;
- revise the angle so the article has a clear point of view;
- adjust the structure to match the angle and available evidence;
- ask the user to resolve a blocking ambiguity;
- explicitly reduce scope until the draft can be written safely.

Avoid treating a not-ready decision as a failure. The checkpoint exists to prevent avoidable draft churn and unsupported expansion.

## Handoff

When the gate is confirmed, hand off:

- the current planning artifacts;
- the decision record;
- any caveats or deferred questions;
- draft-specific instructions such as desired depth, tone, section priorities, or exclusions.

The receiving writer or agent should draft from the confirmed artifacts and visible caveats, not from unstated assumptions.
