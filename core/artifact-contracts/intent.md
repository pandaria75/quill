# Artifact Contract: Intent

## Purpose

The intent artifact clarifies why the article should exist before research, framing, or drafting begins. It gives writers and agents a shared human-readable brief for audience, outcome, scope, and boundaries.

## Recommended Sections

- **Working title or topic**: a short label for the article direction.
- **Audience**: the primary reader, their likely context, and what they already know.
- **Reader need**: the question, problem, or decision the article should help with.
- **Article goal**: the change in understanding, confidence, or action the article should create.
- **Scope**: what the article should cover at this stage.
- **Non-goals**: related topics, claims, or promises the article should avoid.
- **Success criteria**: signs that the article has served the reader well.
- **Constraints and sensitivities**: deadlines, tone expectations, factual risks, confidentiality limits, or terminology preferences.
- **Open questions**: uncertainties that should be resolved before or during later stages.

## Guidance

Keep the intent concise enough to guide decisions without becoming a rigid specification. Prefer explicit tradeoffs over broad ambition: a narrow useful article is usually easier to write and review than an unfocused comprehensive one.

When the audience or goal is uncertain, record the uncertainty rather than smoothing it over. Later artifacts may refine the intent, but they should preserve the stated non-goals unless a human deliberately changes direction.

## Quality Bar

- A new collaborator should be able to explain who the article is for and why it matters.
- The scope and non-goals should reduce predictable drift during angle selection and outlining.
- Success criteria should be observable in the finished article, not only in the writer's intention.
- Sensitive or unsupported claims should be visible early enough to shape research and review.

## Handoff / Relationship To Workflow

The intent artifact typically feeds the context pack and angle stages. It is a recommended checkpoint before committing to a thesis, outline, or draft. If later work changes the audience, goal, or scope, the intent should be updated or accompanied by a short note explaining the change.
