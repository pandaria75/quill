# Artifact Contract: Context Pack

## Purpose

The context pack gathers the minimum useful background for the next writer, reviewer, or agent to continue the article without re-discovering the whole history. It should be compact, current, and focused on decisions that affect the next workflow step.

## Recommended Sections

- **Task or article goal**: the current objective in one or two sentences.
- **Intent summary**: audience, reader need, scope, non-goals, and success criteria.
- **Current stage**: where the work sits in the workflow and what should happen next.
- **Chosen angle or framing**: the active thesis direction, if already selected.
- **Relevant sources and evidence**: source links, notes, or citations that later stages may rely on.
- **Constraints**: tone, length, publication context, confidentiality, terminology, or deadline considerations.
- **Known risks**: factual, legal, brand, security, or reader-misinterpretation risks.
- **Open questions**: unresolved questions and who or what may resolve them.
- **Handoff instructions**: the recommended next action and any stop conditions.

## Guidance

The context pack should summarize and point to supporting artifacts rather than duplicate every note. Prefer current decisions and useful constraints over raw chronology. If a prior decision is no longer active, label it as superseded or omit it.

Context packs may be refreshed between major stages. They should remain platform-neutral and should not assume a specific tool, model provider, storage layer, or execution environment.

## Quality Bar

- A collaborator should be able to continue the next stage without asking for basic orientation.
- The pack should be short enough to read before work begins.
- Risks, unknowns, and stop conditions should be clear rather than hidden in source notes.
- Links or references should help verify claims without turning the pack into a source archive.

## Handoff / Relationship To Workflow

The context pack acts as connective tissue between intent, angle, structure, draft, review, rewrite, final, and memory-candidate work. It is especially useful at handoff points, before review gates, or whenever a different person or agent takes over the article.
