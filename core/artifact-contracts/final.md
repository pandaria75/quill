# Artifact Contract: Final

## Purpose

The final artifact represents the approved article and the minimal handoff context needed for publication, archival, or downstream use. It should distinguish publishable content from notes that are only for collaborators.

## Recommended Sections

- **Final article**: the approved article text in publishable order.
- **Approval status**: who or what role approved the final version, if that is useful for the workflow.
- **Publication notes**: title, subtitle, slug suggestion, excerpt, tags, images, or formatting considerations when relevant.
- **Residual risks or caveats**: accepted limitations, time-sensitive claims, or follow-up checks.
- **Source acknowledgements**: references, credits, or citations that should travel with the article.
- **Change summary**: a brief note on major final changes from the last reviewed draft, if helpful.
- **Post-publication follow-up**: suggested updates, monitoring, or reader-feedback questions.

## Guidance

The final artifact should be clean enough that a publisher or editor can identify the publishable article without guessing. Keep workflow notes separate from the article body. If the target publication format is unknown, provide neutral notes rather than platform-specific instructions.

Final does not mean immutable forever. It means the article has passed the chosen review and approval steps for its current use. Later corrections or updates may create a new final artifact or an addendum.

## Quality Bar

- Publishable content should be clearly separated from handoff notes.
- Known placeholders should be resolved or intentionally documented as accepted caveats.
- Approval context should be visible enough for responsible handoff.
- The artifact should avoid assuming a specific publishing system or runtime.

## Handoff / Relationship To Workflow

The final artifact follows draft, review, and rewrite work. It may feed publishing, archival, or memory-candidate consideration. Only durable lessons or reusable preferences from the completed work should be considered for memory; the full final article should not automatically become memory.
