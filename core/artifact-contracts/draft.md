# Artifact Contract: Draft

## Purpose

The draft artifact holds the current full article text while it is still open to substantial change. It should make the article readable enough to review while preserving visible markers for unresolved issues.

## Recommended Sections

- **Draft status**: first draft, revised draft, review draft, or another plain-language status.
- **Article text**: the full current draft in reading order.
- **Placeholder list**: unresolved facts, examples, citations, screenshots, code snippets, or editorial notes.
- **Known weak spots**: sections the writer believes need special review or rewrite attention.
- **Assumptions**: claims or framing choices that still need confirmation.
- **Review requests**: specific questions for reviewers, such as factual accuracy, audience fit, structure, tone, or completeness.
- **Change notes**: brief notes on major changes since the previous draft, if useful.

## Guidance

The draft should prioritize coherent reading over perfect polish. Placeholders should be easy to find and should describe what is missing, not merely say "fix later." Avoid hiding unresolved claims in polished prose.

Draft artifacts may include comments or notes for collaborators, but those notes should be clearly separated from publishable text. If sensitive or unpublished material appears in the draft, label it clearly and handle it according to the article's confidentiality expectations.

## Quality Bar

- The draft should be complete enough for meaningful review against intent, angle, and structure.
- Unresolved placeholders should be explicit and searchable by a human reader.
- Weak spots should be named so review can focus attention where it matters.
- The draft should not pretend that unverified claims are final.

## Handoff / Relationship To Workflow

The draft artifact follows structure and feeds review. It may cycle through review and rewrite multiple times. Before final handoff, placeholders and known weak spots should be resolved, deliberately accepted, or documented as publication risks.
