# Artifact Contract: Review

## Purpose

The review artifact captures human-readable evaluation of a draft or near-final article. It should separate observations from decisions so writers can understand what to fix, what to keep, and what may block the next workflow step.

## Recommended Sections

- **Review target**: the draft, version, or artifact being reviewed.
- **Review scope**: factual accuracy, audience fit, structure, style, completeness, safety, or another focus.
- **Summary judgment**: a short pass, pass-with-notes, or needs-revision assessment.
- **Blocking issues**: concerns that should be resolved before moving forward.
- **Non-blocking suggestions**: improvements that may help but should not prevent progress.
- **Factual or evidence notes**: claims that need verification, correction, citation, or qualification.
- **Audience and angle notes**: where the draft does or does not serve the intended reader and framing.
- **Structure and flow notes**: gaps, repetition, ordering issues, or unclear transitions.
- **Recommended next action**: rewrite, targeted edit, fact check, stakeholder check, or finalization.

## Guidance

Review should be actionable and proportionate. Name the reason an issue matters, not only the preferred change. Distinguish blockers from preferences so the rewrite stage can prioritize well.

When reviewers disagree, record the disagreement and the decision needed rather than flattening it into a vague compromise. If the review scope was limited, say so explicitly so later collaborators do not over-trust it.

## Quality Bar

- A writer should know what to change next and why.
- Blocking issues should be few, clear, and tied to the article's intent or publication risk.
- Suggestions should avoid rewriting the whole article when a focused change is enough.
- The review should not imply that unreviewed areas have been fully approved.

## Handoff / Relationship To Workflow

The review artifact follows draft and usually feeds rewrite or finalization. It may also support an advisory review gate. Its decisions should be visible enough that later stages can confirm whether blockers were resolved.
