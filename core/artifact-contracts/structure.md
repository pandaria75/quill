# Artifact Contract: Structure

## Purpose

The structure artifact turns intent and angle into a workable article plan. It clarifies the order of ideas, the job of each section, and the evidence or examples needed before drafting begins.

## Recommended Sections

- **Working headline or framing line**: a short reminder of the article's direction.
- **Outline**: major sections and subsections in the recommended reading order.
- **Section jobs**: what each section should accomplish for the reader.
- **Key claims**: important claims, assertions, or explanations associated with each section.
- **Evidence dependencies**: facts, examples, data, quotes, screenshots, or references needed to support the claims.
- **Transitions**: notes on how sections should connect or build on each other.
- **Known gaps**: missing information that may block drafting or require placeholders.
- **Cut list or parking lot**: material that should remain out of the current article unless scope changes.

## Guidance

The outline should guide drafting without forcing final wording too early. Section jobs are often more valuable than polished headings because they explain why a section belongs. When a section does not serve the intent or angle, consider cutting or reframing it before drafting.

It is acceptable to mark uncertain order, unresolved examples, or weak transitions. These notes help later draft and review work focus on real risks instead of treating the outline as finished.

## Quality Bar

- The outline should have a clear beginning, middle, and end for the target reader.
- Each major section should have a distinct purpose.
- Evidence dependencies should be visible enough to prevent unsupported claims in the draft.
- Scope cuts should protect the article from becoming a collection of related notes.

## Handoff / Relationship To Workflow

The structure artifact is the main bridge from angle to draft. It is a recommended input to the before-draft review gate. Drafting may change the structure, but significant changes should be reflected in the structure notes or called out for reviewers.
