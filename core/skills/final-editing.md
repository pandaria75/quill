# Skill: Final Editing

## Purpose

Prepare an already sound article for delivery without flattening its authorial voice or using copy edits to conceal unresolved factual, structural, or reasoning problems.

## When To Use

- at the final advisory gate, after factual and substantive review issues are resolved;
- after narrative polish, style adaptation, and any targeted AI-flavor reduction;
- before publication or delivery handoff.

## Inputs

- reviewed draft;
- rewrite and factual-review notes;
- final gate checklist and any intentional exceptions recorded by the author or user.

## Outputs

- final article;
- a short record of last material changes, retained exceptions, or required user follow-up when useful.

## Editing Boundary

Final editing owns presentation-level clarity, consistency, and accidental residue. It does not decide disputed facts, invent missing context, or rewrite the article into a generic formal voice. When it discovers a substantive issue, route it back to factual review, drafting, or the user rather than guessing a repair.

## Decision Guidance

Classify each issue before editing it.

| Classification | Decision |
| --- | --- |
| Substantive blocking failure | Stop finalization and return it to the appropriate earlier stage or discuss it with the user. |
| Correctable final-edit issue | Fix it when the intended meaning is clear and the change does not alter the author's claim or stance. |
| Tolerable natural irregularity | Leave it when it is understandable, intentional or harmless, and does not create a substantive failure. |

Substantive blocking failures include factual errors, misleading scope, false certainty, severe ambiguity, broken logic, accidental unresolved placeholders, missing context that changes a material conclusion, and formatting that makes essential content unavailable or misleading.

A tolerable natural irregularity can include an occasional slightly awkward but understandable sentence, uneven rhythm, first-person phrasing, or suitable conversational and internet-native wording. It may remain only when it introduces no factual error, severe ambiguity, broken logic, accidental placeholder, or material audience mismatch. Natural texture is permitted; it is never a defect to manufacture.

For example, a slightly rough sentence such as “我当时其实也绕了点路，但这个坑很典型” may fit a reflective technical blog if the surrounding explanation is clear. A sentence whose “this” could refer to two different systems at a critical decision point is severe ambiguity and must not pass merely because it sounds conversational.

## Method

1. Confirm factual review has cleared material claims, or identify and route any issue it missed.
2. Scan headings, links, emphasis, examples, terminology, formatting, and placeholders for accidental inconsistency or residue.
3. Edit grammar and clarity where the intended meaning is clear; preserve first-person stance, deliberate judgments, useful conversational texture, and audience-appropriate internet-native wording.
4. Test each awkward or informal passage for reader comprehension and the substantive-failure boundary. Improve it if it misleads, obscures a material choice, or breaks the reasoning; otherwise do not normalize it solely for polish.
5. Re-read the final flow for intact claims, caveats, and problem-to-why-to-solution reasoning where that progression is used.
6. Record any intentional retained irregularity or unresolved question. Do not label an accidental placeholder as intentional after the fact.

## Stopping And Handoff

Stop and return to factual review or drafting when final editing finds an unsupported claim, factual error, misleading scope, false certainty, severe ambiguity, or broken reasoning. Discuss with the user rather than guessing when resolving the issue depends on unknown facts, authorial intent, audience expectations, public sensitivity, or the intended technical conclusion.

Hand off as final only when no substantive blocking failure remains, formatting supports the intended reading, and any retained natural irregularity remains understandable and non-misleading. If a user decision is still required, hand off the question and affected passage instead of a falsely final version.

## Quality Bar

- the article is ready for its intended handoff without accidental placeholders or misleading presentation;
- formatting and references are consistent enough to support reading and verification;
- first-person voice, clearly marked judgments, and suitable conversational wording remain intact;
- occasional natural irregularity is tolerated only within the substantive-failure boundary.

## Common Failure Modes

- treating copy edits as a substitute for factual or reasoning review;
- flattening first-person voice or conversational wording into uniform formal prose;
- manufacturing roughness, slang, or mistakes in the name of naturalness;
- silently guessing at an unresolved fact, intent, or placeholder;
- calling severe ambiguity or broken logic a style choice.

## Boundaries

This is an advisory, document-first editing method. It does not publish content, run automated checks, resolve facts by model output, or enforce a runtime gate.
