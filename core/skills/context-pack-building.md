# Skill: Context Pack Building

## Purpose

Assemble the minimum working context needed to draft safely and coherently.

## When To Use

- before outline or draft work
- when source material is scattered
- when handoff between people or agents is likely

## Inputs

- audience and intent
- chosen angle
- source notes and constraints

## Outputs

- compact context pack
- source list and key evidence notes
- explicit claim-basis and uncertainty register

## Method

1. Collect only relevant sources and identify what each one can actually support.
2. Summarize the audience, intent, authorial stance, constraints, public-sensitivity concerns, and risks that govern this piece. Use technical-blog terminology: first-person stance (`我`) when useful, visibly marked author judgment, and no simulated experience or certainty.
3. For every material statement, record its basis as a verified fact, evidence-backed conclusion, author judgment, assumption, inference or speculation, or unknown. Include source or reasoning where applicable; a source list alone does not turn an unsupported statement into fact.
4. Give every uncertainty a status: **blocking—discuss with user** or **non-blocking—continue with visible caveat/marker**. Blocking uncertainty includes any uncertainty that could change the central claim, audience, technical conclusion, solution, factual accuracy, public sensitivity, or authorial stance.
5. For a blocking item, stop the affected planning handoff and ask a concise decision question: identify the decision, why it matters, the known evidence or options, and the needed answer. Do not choose a convenient answer on the user's behalf.
6. For a non-blocking item, retain the caveat, placeholder, or open-question marker next to the affected claim and name when it should be resolved. Do not silently promote it into a fact or settled conclusion.
7. Keep the pack short enough to scan quickly; link or summarize evidence instead of copying raw notes.

## Recommended Compact Record

Use a small table or equivalent list when it helps preserve distinctions:

| Item | Basis | Evidence or reasoning | Uncertainty route |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Claim, recommendation, or constraint | fact, conclusion, judgment, assumption, inference/speculation, or unknown | source, observation, or author reasoning | settled; discuss with user; or visible caveat/marker |

This is a document aid, not a runtime schema or a substitute for the shared workflow and advisory gates.

## Quality Bar

- context is sufficient without being bloated
- evidence sources are traceable
- unknowns are explicit
- claim basis remains legible from source through handoff
- blocking decisions are not passed to outlining or drafting as if settled

## Common Failure Modes

- copying raw notes without synthesis
- hiding uncertainty
- adding irrelevant background
- flattening author judgment or inference into a factual summary
- leaving a blocking question as an unmarked handoff risk
