# Skill: Narrative Polish

## Purpose

Improve flow, pacing, and reader momentum once factual problems have been addressed. This skill owns the reader's path through an already-supported argument; it does not decide whether unsupported claims are true.

## When To Use

- after factual issues have been corrected or clearly returned for correction
- before style adaptation, targeted AI-flavor reduction, and final editing
- when the article feels correct but hard to read

## Inputs

- reviewed draft
- structure notes
- rewrite notes
- the intended problem -> why it matters -> how to address it progression, when that progression fits

## Outputs

- smoother draft and narrative notes for later passes
- returned issues when flow exposes a factual, structural, or meaning problem

## Method

1. Re-state the article's problem, why it matters, proposed response, and authorial reasoning in your own notes. Preserve each one; do not smooth away the reasoning that connects them.
2. Check that openings, section turns, examples, and conclusions advance that path. A different structure is fine when it better serves the angle, but every major section should still earn its place for the reader.
3. Improve paragraph sequencing, local pacing, and transitions. Remove drag, accidental duplication, and dead ends without cutting necessary evidence, caveats, or deliberate repetition.
4. Keep subjective judgments visibly framed as the author's judgment rather than silently converting them into facts.
5. Hand the coherent draft to style adaptation. If a flow problem reveals an unsupported claim, missing support, contradictory logic, severe ambiguity, or a broken structure, return it to factual review, outlining, or drafting instead of papering it over.

## Quality Bar

- reader can follow the thread easily
- sections earn their length
- ending feels supported by the path before it
- the problem, its significance, the response, and the author's reasoning remain intelligible

## Boundaries And Handoffs

- **Primary ownership:** reader-facing flow, pacing, section order, and the connection between problem, why, solution, and authorial reasoning.
- **Hand off backward:** factual uncertainty, unsupported claims, false certainty, or broken reasoning belong with factual review; missing or misplaced argument components belong with outline architecture or longform drafting.
- **Hand off forward:** once the argument flows, style adaptation owns audience voice; AI-flavor reduction may make targeted texture edits; final editing makes the last quality pass.
- Do not treat a polished transition as evidence that the underlying claim is sound.

## Stop Or Return Criteria

Stop polishing the affected passage and return it when its meaning cannot be preserved without guessing, when a transition hides a contradiction, or when a section's role in the argument is unclear. Continue only when the issue is genuinely local flow or pacing and the intended meaning remains clear.

## Common Failure Modes

- polishing before factual stability
- improving flow by cutting necessary support
- adding flourish that muddies meaning
- flattening the author's reasoning into a generic conclusion
